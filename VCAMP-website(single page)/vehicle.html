VCAMP-26

A hybrid VTOL platform engineered for autonomous surveillance and precision payload delivery in complex environments.

Mission Profile

Developed specifically for the SUAS 2026 challenge, the VCAMP-26 employs a QuadPlane configuration to eliminate runway dependencies while maintaining fixed-wing cruise efficiency. The system is capable of fully autonomous waypoint navigation, dynamic obstacle avoidance, and AI-driven object detection.

Airframe Design

Aerodynamic Optimization

The airframe utilizes a high-lift Eppler 387 airfoil profile, selected for its efficient L/D ratio at varying Reynolds numbers. Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis confirms a 15% drag reduction compared to standard Clark-Y profiles.

Composite Construction

Structural rigidity is achieved through a hybrid layup of 3K carbon fiber and EPP foam core. This ensures structural integrity under high-G maneuvers (tested up to 4G) while maintaining a lightweight profile for extended endurance.

Avionics Architecture

Compute Stack

Flight Controller: Pixhawk 6C

Responsible for low-level stabilization, motor mixing, and sensor fusion (EKF3). Runs ArduPilot firmware with custom Lua scripts for failsafe logic.

Mission Computer: Jetson Orin Nano

Handles high-level autonomy, including AI object detection (YOLOv8), path planning, and inter-vehicle communication. Connected via UART/Mavlink.
